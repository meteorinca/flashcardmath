
**Civil & Structural Engineering**
Allowable Stress Design (ASD) :: A design method where members are sized so that the calculated stress under service loads does not exceed an allowable stress. [civil-engineering, structural-design]
Slump Test measures :: The workability and consistency of fresh concrete. [civil-engineering, construction, materials]
Purpose of a shear wall :: To resist lateral forces (wind, seismic) and transfer them to the foundation. [civil-engineering, structural, seismic]
Water-Cement Ratio :: The ratio by weight of water to cement in a concrete mix; the single most important factor for strength and durability. [civil-engineering, materials]
The 5 major types of civil engineering :: Structural, Geotechnical, Environmental, Water Resources, Transportation. [civil-engineering]
The bearing capacity of soil is determined by :: Its cohesion, internal friction angle, unit weight, and footing dimensions. [civil-engineering, geotechnical]
PERT Chart purpose :: Program Evaluation and Review Technique - used to schedule, organize, and coordinate tasks within a project. [engineering, project-management]

**Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering**
The First Law of Thermodynamics for a closed system :: ΔU = Q - W (Change in internal energy = Heat added to system - Work done by system). [mechanical-engineering, thermodynamics]
Carnot Efficiency formula :: η = 1 - (T_cold / T_hot), where T is in Kelvin. Represents the maximum possible efficiency of a heat engine. [mechanical-engineering, thermodynamics]
A spur gear transmits power between :: Parallel shafts. [mechanical-engineering, design, machine-elements]
Bernoulli's Principle :: In a flowing fluid, an increase in speed occurs simultaneously with a decrease in pressure or potential energy. [mechanical-engineering, aerospace, fluid-dynamics]
Finite Element Analysis (FEA) :: A numerical method for solving complex engineering problems by breaking them into small, simple elements. [mechanical-engineering, simulation, analysis]
Yield Strength vs. Ultimate Tensile Strength :: Yield strength is the stress at which material begins to deform plastically. Ultimate strength is the maximum stress it can withstand. [mechanical-engineering, materials, properties]
NACA airfoil numbering system :: The "NACA 2412" airfoil: 2% camber at 40% chord, with a 12% thickness-to-chord ratio. [aerospace-engineering, aerodynamics]
Reynolds Number significance :: A dimensionless quantity predicting flow patterns (laminar or turbulent) in a fluid. [mechanical-engineering, fluid-mechanics]

**Electrical & Electronics Engineering**
Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL) :: The sum of all electrical potential differences around any closed network is zero. [electrical-engineering, circuits, laws]
Function of a transistor :: To amplify or switch electronic signals and electrical power. [electrical-engineering, electronics, components]
Thevenin's Theorem :: Any linear electrical network can be replaced by an equivalent circuit with a single voltage source and series resistance. [electrical-engineering, circuits, analysis]
Purpose of a rectifier circuit :: To convert alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC). [electrical-engineering, electronics, power]
Capacitor impedance formula (AC) :: Z_c = 1 / (jωC) where ω is angular frequency. Capacitors block DC and pass AC. [electrical-engineering, electronics]
3-Phase Power advantage over Single-Phase :: More power can be delivered with less conductor material; provides a constant power transfer. [electrical-engineering, power-systems]
Peak vs. RMS voltage for a sine wave :: V_peak = √2 * V_RMS. North American wall voltage is 120V RMS, ~170V peak. [electrical-engineering, basics]

Hazard and Operability Study (HAZOP) :: A structured and systematic examination of a process to identify and evaluate problems. [chemical-engineering, safety]
Brinell Hardness Test :: Measures hardness by pressing a hard steel or carbide ball into a material and measuring the indentation diameter. [materials-engineering, testing]
Phase Diagram shows :: The equilibrium phases of a substance at different temperatures, pressures, and compositions. [materials-engineering, chemical]
A catalyst's primary function :: To increase the rate of a chemical reaction without being consumed in the process. [chemical-engineering, chemistry]
